Upcoming Events

University Hackathon: Innovation and Creativity in Technology

A hackathon is an intense competition where students passionate about technology, programming, and innovation can showcase their skills by developing software or hardware solutions within a limited time. The event starts with a brainstorming session, where teams shape their ideas, followed by an intense period of development and testing. Throughout the competition, participants receive mentorship from industry specialists and university professors, who provide guidance and advice to refine their projects. At the end, each team presents its solution to a jury of professionals and investors, with the chance to win prizes or even turn their idea into a real startup. The hackathon is not just a competition but also an opportunity for networking, developing critical thinking, and gaining hands-on experience. Besides the intellectual challenge, the event also includes relaxation moments with social activities, music, and free food for participants.

Volunteer and Student Opportunities Fair

A volunteer fair is the perfect event for students who want to get involved in extracurricular activities and gain practical experience in various fields. It brings together non-governmental organizations, foundations, and student associations that showcase their projects and offer volunteering opportunities in areas such as education, environmental protection, healthcare, and community development. Students have the chance to speak directly with representatives of these organizations and discover activities that match their interests. In addition to exhibition stands, the fair includes workshops and training sessions where participants can learn useful skills such as time management, leadership, or event organization. Volunteering not only enriches a student’s resume but also provides valuable experiences, connections, and the satisfaction of contributing to an important cause. The event concludes with a networking session, where students can exchange contacts and lay the foundation for future collaborations.

Past Events

Game Night and Socializing for Students

A game night is the perfect event for students who want to unwind and make new friends in a relaxed and fun environment. The event includes a variety of activities, from board games and trivia to console or PC gaming competitions. Participants can form teams and compete in classic games such as Monopoly, Catan, or Uno, as well as popular digital titles. The atmosphere is enhanced with music, snacks, and prizes for the best players, making the evening a memorable experience. Beyond entertainment, the event also has a social purpose, giving students the opportunity to develop teamwork, strategy, and communication skills. Organizers can introduce speed-friending sessions to facilitate interactions between participants so that everyone leaves with new connections. A game night doesn’t require a big budget and can be hosted on campus or in a dedicated space, ensuring success among young people who enjoy competition and good vibes.

Personal Development and Public Speaking Workshop

A personal development workshop is a valuable opportunity for students to improve their communication skills, self-confidence, and ability to speak in public. The event is structured into interactive sessions where participants learn techniques for managing emotions, structuring a speech, and capturing the audience's attention. Invited trainers, whether professors or experienced speakers, provide practical advice and real-life examples to help students overcome their fear of speaking in front of a group. The workshop includes practical exercises, where participants get the chance to practice their skills in front of others and receive constructive feedback. At the end, each student can participate in a small public speaking contest, applying the knowledge they have gained. Besides enhancing communication skills, this type of event helps boost self-confidence and develop a success-oriented mindset, proving extremely useful for future academic presentations or job interviews.
